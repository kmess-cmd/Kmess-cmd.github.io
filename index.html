About Me

I'm Kyle Messer — a cybersecurity professional based in Melbourne, FL with over five years of hands-on experience protecting highly regulated government environments. My career has been built supporting Department of State contractors, where I've worked across the full security operations stack: from vulnerability management and SIEM monitoring to incident response, compliance auditing, and endpoint security.

What drives me is the problem-solving nature of security work — every alert is a puzzle, every vulnerability a risk to be understood and neutralized before it becomes something worse. I hold a Secret clearance and have spent years operating in environments where precision, discretion, and sound judgment aren't optional — they're the job.

Outside of security operations, I'm actively expanding my offensive security skills through TryHackMe and Hack The Box, and exploring the emerging field of AI security through prompt injection research with Lakera. I'm also pursuing my B.S. in Cybersecurity & Information Assurance at Western Governors University, with Network+ and CySA+ on deck.

Before pivoting into cybersecurity, I spent time in aerospace manufacturing, utility operations, and even Walt Disney World — roles that sharpened my attention to detail, ability to work under pressure, and communication skills across every level of an organization. That range of experience gives me a perspective most pure-tech professionals don't have.

When I'm not deep in security work, I'm teaching my kids Python and Arduino — passing on the same curiosity about how things work that led me here in the first place.
